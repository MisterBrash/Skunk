How to play

S K U N K

The game consists of five (5) rounds - S K U N K. Play begins with the "S" column and continues through the "K" column. The object of the game is to accumulate the highest number of points by the end of round "K".

At the beginning of each round, all players are collecting points.

To accumulate points, a pair of dice is rolled. A player gets the total amount of the dice and records it under the current column heading - unless a one (1) comes up.

If a one (1) comes up, play is over for that round and all the player's points in that column are wiped out (unless they locked-in their points - see below).

üêç If "snake eyes" comes up, all points accumulated in prior columns are wiped out as well! (Unless a player has locked-in for that column)

Locking-in: After each roll, a player must decide if they wish to lock-in their points for the current column or to keep accumulating points. This can be done in several ways, for example sitting to lock-in and standing again on the next column or turning a coloured marker over on the table, etc...

If all players have locked-in, double-click the next column heading to continue.

If "allow 1's on first roll" is selected, all players must take the consequences on first rolls of each column.
